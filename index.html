Alan Turing

The father of modern computer science

Alan Turing: A Life Timeline

  • 1912 : Born on June 23, in Maida Vale, London, England.
  • 1926-1931: Attended Sherborne School in Dorset, where he showed early signs of mathematical brilliance.
  • 1931-1934: Studied mathematics at King’s College, Cambridge; graduated with honors.
  • 1936: Published "On Computable Numbers", introducing the concept of a theoretical computing machine—now known as the Turing Machine.
  • 1938: Earned a Ph.D. from Princeton University, working under Alonzo Church.
  • 1939: Joined the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park.
  • 1940-1945: Led efforts to crack the Enigma code using the Bombe, an electromechanical machine he helped design.
  • 1945: Worked on the design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine) at the National Physical Laboratory.
  • 1948: Joined University of Manchester, contributing to early computer development and artificial intelligence concepts.
  • 1950: Published “Computing Machinery and Intelligence”, proposing the Turing Test for machine intelligence.
  • 1952: Arrested and convicted for homosexuality; subjected to chemical castration as punishment.
  • 1954: Died on June 7, from cyanide poisoning in Wilmslow, Cheshire; widely believed to be suicide.
  • 2009: British Prime Minister Gordon Brown issued an official apology on behalf of the UK government.
  • 2013: Official posthumous pardon granted by Queen Elizabeth II.
  • 2021: Alan Turing's face featured on the Bank of England’s £50 note, celebrating his lasting legacy.
